Foursquare has inked a major tech integration deal with Twitter that will give the "check in" fresh life, albeit under a different name. Starting today, Twitter users can add a location to their tweets, choosing from a drop-down menu of possible places you are. In many cases, specific businesses or destinations will appear as choices, a service powered by Foursquare. For Foursquare, that's potentially huge because Twitter now becomes a source of incoming data on where smart phone users are frequenting. As user growth stalled in the original Foursquare, prompting 2014's shift to a search-based Foursquare and the new social app Swarm, the original source of that data — the "check in" — waned.
